BACKGROUND: 
Acquired copper deficiency (hypocupremia) mimics vitamin B12 deficiency, presenting with severe hematological cytopenias and subacute combined degeneration-like myelopathy. Current interventions often rely on compounded mixtures with variable bioavailability. This study evaluates an unmixed, high-purity Cupric (Cu2+) formulation designed for optimized systemic delivery.

OBJECTIVES: 
1. To quantify the rate of serum copper and ceruloplasmin normalization over a 12-week therapeutic cycle.
2. To evaluate the resolution of neutropenia and macrocytic anemia metrics.
3. To determine structural safety thresholds by tracking liver function markers (ALT/AST) to eliminate toxicity risks.

METHODS: 
This single-center, open-label, phase II investigator-initiated trial intends to enroll 45 adult patients exhibiting documented serum copper levels < 60 µg/dL. Participants will receive a controlled dual-delivery regimen consisting of [Insert Formula Volume/Mg] under direct integrative medicine specialist observation. Serum copper, ceruloplasmin, complete blood counts (CBC), and hepatic panels will be gathered at baseline, weekly for 4 weeks, and bi-weekly thereafter.

EXPECTED OUTCOMES: 
It is anticipated that 85% of participants will achieve safe physiological target ranges (serum copper 70–140 µg/dL) by week 4 without presenting elevated ALT/AST values exceeding 2x the Upper Limit of Normal (ULN). Secondary outcomes include quantifiable improvements in gait stability and absolute neutrophil counts.

CLINICAL RELEVANCE: 
Establishing a safe, unmixed single-agent protocol provides university clinics with a highly standardized approach to trace mineral imbalances, removing compounding variables that interfere with public research data.
