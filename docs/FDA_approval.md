There are exactly two FDA-approved indications for pharmaceutical-grade, single-entity copper to treat human deficiencies. The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) strictly limits the therapeutic use of regulated copper to specific clinical environments and delivery pathways. [1, 2]

* * * * *

1\. Total Parenteral Nutrition (TPN) Supplementation
----------------------------------------------------

-   Approved Substance: Cupric Chloride Injection, USP. [3, 4]
-   Indication: Indicated for use as a supplement to intravenous solutions administered for Total Parenteral Nutrition (TPN). [4]
-   Clinical Purpose: This formulation is used to maintain baseline serum copper levels, prevent the depletion of endogenous trace mineral stores, and suppress the onset of secondary systemic deficiency symptoms (such as leukopenia, neutropenia, microcytic/macrocytic anemia, and osteoporosis). [1, 4]
-   Administration Rule: It is strictly intended as an additive diluted into a large-volume parenteral solution. Direct, un-diluted intravenous or intramuscular injection is absolutely contraindicated due to tissue irritation caused by its acidic pH (~2.0). [4, 5, 6]

2\. Congenital Copper Transport Deficiency (Menkes Disease)
-----------------------------------------------------------

-   Approved Substance: Zycubo (Copper Histidinate) Subcutaneous Injection. [7, 8]
-   Indication: Indicated for the treatment of Menkes disease in pediatric patients. [8]
-   Clinical Purpose: Menkes disease is a rare, fatal, X-linked neurodegenerative disorder caused by a genetic mutation in the *ATP7A* copper transporter gene, which fundamentally destroys the body's ability to absorb copper through the gastrointestinal tract. This specialized subcutaneous injection acts as a targeted replacement therapy that completely bypasses the impaired gut mucosa to deliver bioavailable copper directly into systemic circulation. [7, 9]
-   Limitation of Use: This pharmaceutical formulation is explicitly *not* indicated for the treatment of Occipital Horn Syndrome (a milder allelic variant of *ATP7A* mutations). [8]

* * * * *

💡 Crucial Regulatory Distinctions
----------------------------------

-   Cupric Sulfate Is Discontinued: Single-entity, pharmaceutical-grade *cupric sulfate* injections are no longer actively marketed as standalone FDA-approved products. Its use for TPN is now predominantly handled via fixed trace-element combinations (like *Tralement*) or via *cupric chloride*.
-   Oral Formulations: Oral options (such as copper gluconate or copper sulfate tablets) are widely distributed under [FDA Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act (DSHEA)](https://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/Copper-HealthProfessional/) guidelines. They hold standard Daily Values (DV) but are classified as nutritional supplements, meaning they do not possess individual FDA prescription drug indication approval for disease treatment.
-   Investigational Exclusions: Popular copper peptides used in compounding settings (such as *GHK-Cu*) have no FDA-approved indications for copper repletion or clinical deficiencies and face heavy regulatory scrutiny for systemic injection use. [2, 3, 10, 11, 12, 13]
