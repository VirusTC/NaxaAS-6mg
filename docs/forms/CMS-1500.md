1\. Explicit Billing Modifier Guidelines (Form CMS-1500)

Accurate application of billing modifiers on the Form CMS-1500 ensures transparent documentation of specialized, investigational, or split-care scenarios involving cupric compounds.

Key Modifiers for Cupric Treatment Integration

-   **Modifier 25 (Significant, Separately Identifiable Evaluation and Management Service):**
    -   **Application:** Append to the Evaluation and Management (E/M) code (e.g., 99213 or 99214) when an integrative medicine physician performs a comprehensive metabolic/deficiency safety evaluation on the same day as an outpatient intravenous copper infusion.
    -   **CMS-1500 Placement:** Enter in Item 24D, adjacent to the E/M procedure code.
-   **Modifier Q1 (Routine Science/Clinical Trial Service):**
    -   **Application:** Append to all routine evaluation, laboratory, and standard care codes associated with the approved Investigator-Initiated Trial (IIT). This signals to payers that the routine costs are covered under qualifying clinical trial guidelines.
    -   **CMS-1500 Placement:** Enter in Item 24D under the modifier column for matching rows.
-   **Modifier JW (Drug Amount Discarded/Not Administered):**
    -   **Application:** If a single-dose vial of white-labeled compounding cupric solution is opened but only a partial volume is infused to match a patient's strictly calculated bioavailable upper limit constraint, use this modifier on a separate line item to report the exact discarded amount.
-   **Modifier JZ (Zero Drug Amount Discarded/Not Administered):**
    -   **Application:** Must be appended to the compound supply billing code if the entire single-use vial or calculated dosage unit is completely administered, leaving zero waste.
