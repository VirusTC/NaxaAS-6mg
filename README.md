# NaxaAS-6mg
Cuperic combats mold and mildew primarily through contact killing and cellular disruption. When fungi interact with copper, the metal releases Cu⁺ and Cu²⁺ ions that destabilize cell membranes, induce oxidative stress, denature proteins, and interfere with DNA replication, effectively preventing spore germination.

Clinical Mechanisms to Fight Fungi
----------------------------------

The positively charged copper ions (Cu²⁺) act through multiple overlapping multisite mechanisms:

-   Membrane Permeabilization: Ions disrupt the fungal lipid bilayer, causing structural damage and leakage of intracellular contents.
-   Oxidative Stress: The release of ions causes lipid peroxidation and triggers reactive oxygen species (ROS) production, damaging fungal organelles.
-   Protein and Nucleic Acid Alteration: Copper binds to functional groups of fungal enzymes and denatures nucleic acids, severely disrupting cellular metabolism.
-   Ergosterol Disruption: Studies show copper ions can penetrate the fungal membrane and disrupt the biosynthesis of ergosterol---a key fungal membrane component.

Copper Delivery Methods
-----------------------

-   Oral: Used to correct copper deficiency (which supports immune function and red blood cell formation). Common oral forms include copper gluconate, copper sulfate, and copper histidinate.
-   Intravenous (IV): Typically administered as copper chloride or copper sulfate and used in parenteral nutrition to prevent deficiency in patients unable to absorb nutrients via the gastrointestinal tract.
-   Medical Foods: Products like copper-enriched powders or specialized enteral formulas are designed to provide nutritional support for specific metabolic or deficiency disorders (e.g., Menkes disease).

IRB Compassionate Use Cases
---------------------------

Compassionate use (expanded access) is regulated by the FDA to allow patients with serious or immediately life-threatening conditions access to investigational products outside of clinical trials. While copper itself is an FDA-approved essential nutrient, investigational copper-based chelators or experimental copper-alloy devices may fall under compassionate use rules:

-   Copper Chelator Therapy: Compassionate access has been evaluated for the use of copper chelators (such as tetrathiomolybdate) in carefully monitored studies to treat rare, severe fungal infections by restricting copper trafficking to the fungi.
-   Experimental Antimicrobial Devices: Some hospital IRBs manage trials and compassionate/emergency placements of copper-alloy environmental surfaces and experimental copper-impregnated textiles designed to reduce hospital-acquired fungal and bacterial infections.
